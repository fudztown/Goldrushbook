Chapter Previews

Chapter 1: Introduction - The Modern Gold Rush

"In the modern business landscape, the gold rush isn't about finding literal gold—it's about discovering where value truly exists in your organization."

The gold rush of the 1800s was characterized by prospectors who left everything behind in search of fortune. Today's business leaders are modern prospectors, searching for value in increasingly complex organizational landscapes.

This chapter introduces the core concept that will guide you through this book: just as gold miners needed to develop systematic approaches to find and extract gold efficiently, today's organizations need frameworks to identify, organize around, deliver, and measure value.

Chapter 2: Organizing Around Value

"The most successful organizations don't just find gold—they organize their entire operation around extracting it efficiently."

Traditional organizational structures often create silos that impede value flow. This chapter explores how to transition from functional hierarchies to value stream networks that optimize the end-to-end delivery of value to customers.

You'll discover how companies like Amazon and Toyota have reorganized their operations to focus on value streams, resulting in dramatic improvements in efficiency, innovation, and customer satisfaction.

Chapter 3: The Value Framework

"A comprehensive value framework is your map to the gold fields—without it, you're just digging randomly and hoping for luck."

This chapter introduces the complete Value Framework with its four interconnected components: Value Identification, Value Organization, Value Delivery, and Value Measurement.

Learn how these components work together to create a virtuous cycle of continuous improvement and value creation. The framework provides a practical approach that can be applied to organizations of any size and in any industry.

Chapter 4: Measuring What Matters

"If you can't measure it, you can't improve it. But contrary to popular belief, everything that matters can be measured."

Drawing on Douglas Hubbard's groundbreaking work in Applied Information Economics, this chapter challenges the common misconception that certain things are "immeasurable" and provides practical techniques for measuring even the most intangible aspects of value.

You'll learn how to apply calibrated estimation, decomposition, and other measurement techniques to reduce uncertainty and make better decisions about where to invest your organization's resources.
